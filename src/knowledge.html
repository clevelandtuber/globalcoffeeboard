🫘 Arabica vs Robusta

Almost all coffee traded in the world comes from two species. Arabica (Coffea arabica) is prized for its sweetness, acidity and aromatic complexity. It grows at higher altitudes (900–2,200 m), is more delicate, and commands a premium — it's the NY "C" contract (KC=F), quoted in US cents per pound.

Robusta (Coffea canephora) is hardier, grows at lower altitudes, resists disease, and carries roughly twice the caffeine. It gives body and crema — the backbone of espresso blends and instant coffee. It trades on ICE London (RC=F), quoted in US dollars per tonne.

India is unusual: it grows both, and its Robusta is world-renowned for quality — much of it shade-grown alongside pepper and cardamom.

💧 Processing methods

How the fruit is removed from the seed shapes the cup as much as the variety does.

  • Washed (wet): fruit removed before drying — clean, bright, high-clarity cups.
  • Natural (dry): cherries dried whole — fruity, heavy-bodied, wine-like.
  • Honey / pulped natural: some fruit mucilage left on — a sweet middle ground.
  • Monsooned Malabar: a uniquely Indian process where beans are exposed to monsoon winds, mellowing acidity into a deep, musty richness.

🔥 Roast & brew

Roast level moves flavour from bright and acidic (light) to bold and bittersweet (dark). Match the brew to the bean:

  • Espresso: fine grind, ~9 bars, 25–30s — Robusta adds crema and body.
  • Pour-over / filter: medium grind — showcases Arabica clarity.
  • French press: coarse grind, full immersion — rich and full-bodied.
  • South Indian filter coffee: a fine dark roast with chicory, brewed in a metal filter and served with hot milk.

🇮🇳 Indian coffee

India is the world's 7th–8th largest coffee producer, and grows ~70% Robusta and ~30% Arabica. Coffee is famously grown under shade, making Indian coffee a haven for biodiversity.

  • Karnataka — ~70% of national output (Kodagu/Coorg, Chikmagalur, Hassan).
  • Kerala — predominantly Robusta (Wayanad, Travancore).
  • Tamil Nadu — Arabica in the Nilgiris, Pulneys and Shevaroys.
  • "Non-traditional" & NE India — Andhra Pradesh, Odisha and the North-East.
The Coffee Board of India (Ministry of Commerce) publishes the Daily Coffee Market Report of farm-gate prices by grade — the reference we track on the dashboard.

📈 Prices & markets

World coffee prices are set on two futures markets and translated to local currency:

  • The "C" price — the ICE NY Arabica benchmark (KC=F), the reference for most Arabica trade, in ¢/lb.
  • London Robusta — the ICE Europe Robusta benchmark (RC=F), in $/tonne.
  • Differential — the premium or discount a specific origin/grade trades at versus the benchmark, reflecting quality, logistics and demand.
  • FX (USD/INR) — because benchmarks are in dollars, the rupee rate directly changes what a farmer earns.

Our dashboard combines all four: benchmark → convert with FX → compare to the local Coffee Board price → a differential and a plain-language verdict.

🌾 For farmers

Use the dashboard to see today's ₹/kg, the sell-or-hold verdict, and the earnings calculator to value your harvest. Track the differential: a strong local premium is often a good moment to sell.

💼 For café owners & entrepreneurs

Green-bean cost is the foundation of your cup economics. Watch benchmark trends and FX to time purchasing, and use processing/variety knowledge to build a menu story customers pay for.

🚢 For exporters

Track the benchmark, differential and FX together. A negative local differential plus a firm benchmark can make export contracts more attractive; a rising rupee erodes dollar receipts.

📖 Glossary

  • Parchment: coffee still in its dried inner husk (before hulling).
  • Cherry: the dried whole coffee fruit.
  • AB grade: a common Indian bean-size grade.
  • Green beans: unroasted, processed coffee ready for trade.
  • Crema: the golden foam on a well-pulled espresso.
  • Cupping: the standardised tasting used to score coffee.
